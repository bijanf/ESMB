\chapter{Code Map: Module to Chapter}\label{chap:appC_codemap}

This appendix is a directory: it maps every source module in the \code{chronos\_esm/}
package to the chapter(s) of this book that develop its theory and numerics, with a
one-line statement of the module's role in the model. The intent is to let a reader who
is staring at a piece of code jump to the place where it is explained --- and,
conversely, to let a reader who has just absorbed a chapter find the file that turns
those equations into running \jax. Where a single module spans more than one chapter
(\code{veros\_driver.py}, for instance, touches the ocean equations, the mixing
parameterizations, and the numerics) all relevant chapter links are given.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Honesty note.}\quad \model{} is research software, and the package
contains code at three distinct maturity levels. (i) \emph{Live} modules are wired into
the integrated model and validated against observations. (ii) \emph{Standalone /
prototype} modules (the prognostic-momentum ocean core, the differentiable \dino{}
coupler) are validated against analytic balances but not yet the default path; they are
the building blocks of the working-ESM roadmap. (iii) \emph{Dead / superseded} modules
(\code{atmos/qtcm.py}, the \code{jcm} adapter) are kept only for historical reference.
The status column below states which is which, so the reader is never misled about what
the code actually does.

\section{Ocean}
% ===================================================================

The ocean is the most developed component. The \emph{live} ocean is the
z-level, diagnostic-velocity driver in \code{veros\_driver.py} (Chapters
\ref{chap:ch04_ocean_equations}--\ref{chap:ch06_ocean_numerics}); the \emph{prototype}
prognostic-momentum core (\code{barotropic.py}, \code{momentum.py}) and the interim
thermohaline closure (\code{overturning.py}) are the subject of Chapters
\ref{chap:ch07_ocean_prognostic_core}--\ref{chap:ch08_ocean_overturning}.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.27\textwidth} p{0.16\textwidth} p{0.49\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Module} & \textbf{Chapter(s)} & \textbf{Role / status} \\
\midrule
\code{ocean/veros\_driver.py} & \ref{chap:ch04_ocean_equations}, \ref{chap:ch05_ocean_mixing}, \ref{chap:ch06_ocean_numerics} & Z-level primitive-equation ocean: tracer ($\Temp,\Salt$) advection-diffusion (RK4), diagnostic thermal-wind $\uvec$, high-lat salinity sponge. The \emph{live} ocean. \\
\code{ocean/mixing.py} & \ref{chap:ch05_ocean_mixing} & Isopycnal (Redi) diffusion + Gent--McWilliams eddy parameterization with differentiable slope limiters \citep{gentmcwilliams1990}. \emph{Live}. \\
\code{ocean/solver.py} & \ref{chap:ch06_ocean_numerics} & AD-safe conjugate-gradient elliptic solver for $\diver(H^{-1}\grad\psib)=\vort$ (variable-coefficient invert). \emph{Live}. \\
\code{ocean/bathymetry.py} & \ref{chap:ch06_ocean_numerics} & Turns a depth field $\dep(\lon,\lat)$ into static 3-D wet masks (full-cell ``staircase'' coasts/floor) for no-flux boundaries \citep{griffies2004}. \emph{Live}. \\
\code{ocean/barotropic.py} & \ref{chap:ch07_ocean_prognostic_core} & Prototype prognostic barotropic vorticity core: $\dt{\vort}=-\beq\,\pd{\psib}{x}-r\vort+F+\nu\lap\vort$. Standalone, validated vs analytic gyres. \emph{Prototype}. \\
\code{ocean/momentum.py} & \ref{chap:ch07_ocean_prognostic_core} & Prototype prognostic baroclinic momentum: time-integrated $\dt{\uvec}$ with Coriolis, hydrostatic PGF from $\dens$, friction. Standalone, validated vs thermal wind. \emph{Prototype}. \\
\code{ocean/overturning.py} & \ref{chap:ch08_ocean_overturning} & Interim Stommel/Marotzke box-style thermohaline closure giving $\dens$ a pathway to a coherent AMOC \citep{stommel1961,marotzke1991}. \emph{Interim closure}. \\
\code{ocean/diagnostics.py} & \ref{chap:ch08_ocean_overturning} & Meridional / Atlantic overturning streamfunctions and the AMOC@$26.5^\circ$N scalar metric (in $\Sv$). \emph{Live}. \\
\code{ocean/flux\_correction.py} & \ref{chap:ch13_coupler_fluxes}, \ref{chap:ch14_forcing_response} & SST flux correction: Haney restoring (control) vs.\ frozen q-flux $+$ weak anomaly restoring (forcing-responsive free run). \emph{Prototype}. \\
\code{ocean/utils.py} & \ref{chap:ch03_differentiable} & Differentiable \code{soft\_clip} (tanh) replacing hard clips so gradients survive at bounds. \emph{Utility}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Ocean modules. The live ocean uses diagnostic velocities; the prognostic core
is the standalone successor under development.}
\end{table}

\section{Atmosphere}
% ===================================================================

Two atmosphere paths coexist. The \emph{multi-level} path wraps Google's \dino{}
spectral primitive-equation dycore (Chapter \ref{chap:ch09_atmosphere_multilevel}) and
is the active control-run atmosphere. The \emph{single-level} barotropic path (Chapter
\ref{chap:ch10_atmosphere_singlelevel}) is the one inside the legacy
\code{main.step\_coupled}. The \code{qtcm.py} adapter is superseded.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{0.27\textwidth} p{0.16\textwidth} p{0.49\textwidth}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Module} & \textbf{Chapter(s)} & \textbf{Role / status} \\
\midrule
\code{atmos/dino\_atmos.py} & \ref{chap:ch09_atmosphere_multilevel}, \ref{chap:ch18_jets_stormtracks}, \ref{chap:ch19_tropics_monsoon} & Multi-level atmosphere on the \dino{} dycore (T31, $\sigma$-levels, ImEx-RK3): SST-coupled Held--Suarez forcing $+$ prognostic moisture \citep{heldsuarez1994,kochkov2024neuralgcm}. \emph{Active control-run atmosphere}. \\
\code{atmos/spectral.py} & \ref{chap:ch09_atmosphere_multilevel}, \ref{chap:ch10_atmosphere_singlelevel} & Spherical-harmonic / FFT spectral transforms and the (unused) Helmholtz solver for the single-level dynamics. \emph{Support / partially live}. \\
\code{atmos/dynamics.py} & \ref{chap:ch10_atmosphere_singlelevel} & Single-level (barotropic) primitive-equation step: $\vort$, divergence, $\Temp$, $\ln p_s$, advected $q$/$\mathrm{CO_2}$; wind relaxed to a climatological jet; strong hyperdiffusion. \emph{Live (legacy path)}. \\
\code{atmos/physics.py} & \ref{chap:ch10_atmosphere_singlelevel}, \ref{chap:ch14_forcing_response} & Single-level physics: \softplus{} precipitation (convection), radiative cooling, $\mathrm{CO_2}$ forcing \citep{myhre1998}. \emph{Live (legacy path)}. \\
\code{atmos/qtcm.py} & \ref{chap:ch10_atmosphere_singlelevel} & Quasi-equilibrium tropical circulation stepper. \emph{Superseded / dead}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Atmosphere modules. The \dino{} multi-level path is active for control runs;
the single-level path drives the legacy coupled loop.}
\end{table}

\section{Coupler}

The two \code{dino\_*} modules are the differentiable multi-level coupling path

\section{Where the climate test cases live}
% ===================================================================

The canonical experiments of Part~IV are driven from the \code{experiments/} directory
rather than the package itself, but they exercise specific modules. The AMOC
hysteresis/tipping case (Chapter \ref{chap:ch17_amoc_tipping}) drives
\code{ocean/overturning.py}, \code{ocean/diagnostics.py}, and
\code{ocean/flux\_correction.py}; the jets-and-storm-tracks case (Chapter
\ref{chap:ch18_jets_stormtracks}) and the tropics/monsoon case (Chapter
\ref{chap:ch19_tropics_monsoon}) both exercise \code{atmos/dino\_atmos.py} via the
\dino{} control harness \citep{vallis2017}. The forcing-response experiments (Chapter
\ref{chap:ch14_forcing_response}) couple \code{forcing.py}, \code{atmos/physics.py}, and
\code{ocean/flux\_correction.py}.

\begin{remark}
The single source of truth is the code itself. Where this map and a module's docstring
disagree about status, trust the docstring --- the package evolves faster than the book,
and each module's leading docstring states its current maturity and known limitations.
\end{remark}
